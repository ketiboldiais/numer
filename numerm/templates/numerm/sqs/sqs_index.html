Sequences & Series: Prelude

This volume explores sequences and series. In the typical U.S. math sequence, sequences and series are covered in a course called Calculus II. However, in my experience (both personally and surveying course reviews), the sequences and series module in Calculus II appears almost out of nowhere and entirely unrelated to calculus. Only later in the calculus course do the connections begin creeping in.

Because of this abruption, sequences and series are treated in a separate volume. And rightly so; they require a shift in thinking, and are important to not just calculus, but a variety of other mathematical fields. Furthermore, the benefit to online formatted text is the availability of hyperlinking. With a physical textbook, separating sequences and series entirely would require students to purchase separate textbooks. No such cost appears here, so the liberty to separate the topics entirely is at our disposal. Accordingly, there are portions of the calculus volume that link directly to these materials.

Sequences and series, however, can be studied in their own right. As we'll see, sequences and series is one of the oldest and most developed topics in mathematics, and its impacts on mathematics, the sciences, and technology is undisputed.
