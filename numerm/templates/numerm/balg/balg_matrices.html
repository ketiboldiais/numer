A few key observations from the analysis above: First, given the matrices ${\textbf{A} = n \times m}$ matrix and ${\textbf{B} = a \times b,}$ when we compute ${\textbf{A} \times \textbf{B},}$ we are actually trying to construct an ${(n \times m) \times (a \times b)}$ matrix. To do so, it must be the case that ${m}$ and ${a}$ are equal. If ${m = a,}$ then we say that ${\textbf{A}}$ and ${\textbf{B}}$ are conformable for multiplication. If ${m \neq a,}$ then we cannot compute the product matrix ${\textbf{A} \times \textbf{B}.}$ If we can compute the product matrix ${\textbf{A} \times \textbf{B},}$ the order of the resulting product matrix is given by ${n \times b.}$
