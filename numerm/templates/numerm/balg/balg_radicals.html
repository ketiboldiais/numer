The Square Root. Suppose we have a number ${a}$ where ${a \in \R^{+}.}$ The square root of ${a}$ is the number that, when multiplied by itself, i.e., ${a^2,}$ returns ${a.}$ Now, that number may be positive or negative, because squaring two numbers, positive or negative, yields a positive number.

Definition. Given ${a \in \R^{+},}$ ${\sqrt{a}}$ is the number ${n,}$ where ${n \in \R}$ such that ${n^2 = a.}$

From the square root definition, we can begin deriving rules:

  • Let ${a, b \in \R_{\geq 0}}$
  • Assumption
  • ${(\sqrt{a})^2 (\sqrt{b})^2 = ab}$
  • Definition of Square Root
  • ${(\sqrt{a})^2 (\sqrt{b})^2 = (\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b})^2}$
  • The Power Product Rule of Exponents
  • ${(\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b})^2 = ab}$
  • The Transitive Property of Equality
  • ${\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} \in \R_{\geq 0} }$
  • The product of non-negative numbers is non-negative
  • ${ab \in \R_{\geq 0}}$
