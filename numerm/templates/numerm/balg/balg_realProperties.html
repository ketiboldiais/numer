Properties of Real Numbers

The fundamental properties of real numbers are the rules grounding algebraic manipulation. From these properties, we can derive a wide variety of mathematical results.

Exercise. Compute ${1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \ldots + 98 + 99 + 100.}$

Solution

There are 100 terms in this problem. The first term can be paired and added with the last term, the second term with the second to last, the third with the third to last, etc.: ${(1 + 100) + (2 + 99) + (3 + 98) \ldots + (49 + 52) + (50 + 51).}$ Each of these pairs sums to 101. There are 50 pairs total, so the sum is: ${50 \cdot 101 = 5050.}$ This computation is attributed to the great mathematician Karl Gauss while he was a schoolboy.
