Transcendental Functions

The transcendental functions are the functions that are not algebraic functions. This family of functions includes the trigonometric, inverse trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions, as well as a gargantuan number of functions that have yet to be named.

Some examples of such functions:

  • ${f(x) = \sin x}$
  • ${g(t) = 2^t}$
  • ${h(r) = \log_{3}r}$

Transcendental functions are mentioned here to acknowledge their existence early on, but a thorough treatment is provided in the Calculus title, where they are explored as sums of infinite series.
