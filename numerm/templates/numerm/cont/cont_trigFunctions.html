Trigonometric Functions

Now, in the context of geometry and discrete mathematics, sine, cosine, tangent, and their reciprocals are purely ratios. However, in continuous mathematics, we will think of these ratios as trigonometric functions. The distinction is made because we seldom treat the trigonometric ratios as purely ratios in continuous mathematics; rather, they more often arise as functions.

(3) There are three pairs of reciprocals: ${(\cos \theta, \sec \theta),}$ ${(\sin \theta, \csc \theta),}$ and ${(\tan \theta, \cot \theta).}$

From the above, we know the following: The point ${(0, 1)}$ is ${(\cos(\pi/2), \sin(\pi/2)),}$ ${(1, 0)}$ is ${(\cos 0, \sin \pi),}$ ${(0, -1)}$ is ${(\cos (\pi/2), \sin(-\pi/2)),}$ and ${(-1, 0)}$ is ${(\cos (-\pi), \sin(-\pi)).}$
