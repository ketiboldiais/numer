Determining the Area of a Sector

Now that we know how to compute arc length, we can use this knowledge to compute the area of a sector of a circle. A sector is a region of a circle bound by two radii and and the subtending arc. In other words, the formal term for a slice of pizza is a pizza sector (assuming the pizza is circular and the pizza is sliced traditionally). Each side of the slice is a radius of length ${r,}$ and the crust is an arc of length ${s.}$

How do we determine the area of a sector? First, the area of a circle is given by the formula ${A = \pi r^2.}$ This is the area of a full circle. So, in terms of an angle that subtends the entire circle, the area of such a sector if ${A = (2 \pi / 2 \pi) \pi r^2.}$ But what of some angle ${\theta}$ that does not fully subtend the cricle? To calculate the sector formed by this angle, we would be computing a fraction of the area of the full circle.
