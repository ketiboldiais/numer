Composition of Functions

Functions are not required to take numbers. They can also take other functions. The process of passing a function as an input to another function is called function composition.

For example, consider the function:

This a function composition. $f(x) = \sqrt{(x+h)^2-3(x+h)}$, and in the equation above, takes an input of $x + h$. Because the function takes an expression, we can expand and simply the function:

The equation above is a portion of an important formula in mathematics called the difference quotient:

The difference quotient formula gives us the relationship of the slope of a line. The factor $f(x)$ represents the y-coordinate, or output, of a point on the line. The factor $f(x+h)$ represents another y-coordinate, or output, of a point on the line. The divisor $h$ represents the distance between $x$, the input for $f(x)$, and $x+h$, the input for $f(x+h)$.
