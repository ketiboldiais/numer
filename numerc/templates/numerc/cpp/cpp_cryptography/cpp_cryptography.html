Cryptography

Cryptography is the practice and study of securing communications in the presence of third parties. Cryptography has a long and rich history. From Julius Caesar to the Navajo Code Talkers, so long as we've felt the need to keep communications private, cryptography sprung like Athena from Zeus's head. In the modern era, we've come to rely on cryptography for many of the things we take for granted: Transferring funds electronically, storing user information, signing contracts, assessing electronic evidence, securing medical records, emailing, text messaging, uploading files, downloading files, the list is endless. This article presents an overview of the fundamentals of cryptography by investigating standard techniques and practices.

Basic Terminology

To begin, we define a few pieces of basic terminology.

  1. Plaintext is the message to be encrypted.
  2. Ciphertext is the message encrypted.
  3. Encryption is the process of encoding — whether it be scrambling, rewriting, symbol-rearranging, masking, etc. — a message in such a way that only the intended and authorized recipients can access the message.
  4. Decryption is the process of decoding the message.
  5. The key is the sequence needed for both encryption and decryption.
